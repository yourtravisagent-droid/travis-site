Philosophy

Quiet by default

Most assistants try to impress you with activity. Travis is the opposite. It runs in the background, does the work, and only interrupts you when a decision you haven't delegated actually requires your attention.

The bar for sending you a Telegram is high. The bar for making a purchase is a real tap. The bar for reaching outside your household is higher still. Defaults tilt toward "do the right thing silently," but every irreversible action goes through explicit approval.

Runs on your Mac

No cloud account, no Travis-the-SaaS, no data-sharing tier. A Mac you already own, a repo you already control, secrets in your own Keychain. Agents talk to sites and APIs through your own sessions — they're doing what you would do, just faster and more patiently.

Architecturally boring by design. launchd for scheduling, Playwright for sites that don't have APIs, Telegram for inputs, Claude for judgment calls.

One tap is the UI

Travis is not an app. There's no dashboard you log into. There are no notifications you sort through. The interface is a Telegram chat — one thread, three kinds of messages: a briefing, a decision prompt, a confirmation. Approve or decline, and it keeps going.
